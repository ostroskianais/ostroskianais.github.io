ABOUT ME


I am a PhD student in Environmental Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh. My previous degree was obtained at Universidade Federal do Paraná, in Brazil, where I am originally from! The main goal of my research is to explore food systems and the connections within them: production and consumption, environmental impacts, resource use, dependency on different types of animals.

I am passionate about leveraging data to answer sustainability and complex systems questions. I have gained a lot of skills throughout the years: programming in R and Python, statistics, machine learning, linear optimization, network analysis, remote sensing with Google Earth Engine in Javascript, and geospatial analysis. I believe my greatest strength is in the diversity and range of my quantitative skills and how I am able to leverage them to solve problems and answer questions.

Personally, I like to paint abstracts, roller skate, make data visualizations, explore new cities, listen to Taylor Swift, learn about art and fashion, and pet all the pets.
