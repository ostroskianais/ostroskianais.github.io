Anaís Ostroski


I am a Postdoctoral scholar at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab, in the Systems and Energy Techonologies Analysis division in the Energy Technologies Area


My research is at the intersection of energy systems, supply chains, and the environment. I tackle sustainability challenges with a variety of methods - mainly complex systems analysis, data science, optimization, network science, and remote sensing. I hold a Ph.D. in Civil & Environmental Engineering from the University of Pittsburgh, and a M.S. and B.S. in Environmental Engineering from Federal University of Paraná, Brazil.

Research Interests




My research focuses on developing computational and data-driven approaches to understand, model, and optimize environmental sustainability in complex, interconnected systems. I combine large-scale data, network models, optimization, and remote sensing methods to study how material flows, supply chains, and energy infrastructure systems shape environmental and human outcomes, with the goal of informing more effective and transparent sustainability decisions.

My research interests are organized around four interconnected themes:




1. Environmental systems modeling

A central focus of my research is the implications of human activity on emissions, nutrient cycling, land-use, and ecological systems. I am especially interested in hard-to-abate sectors, such as agriculture and cement, to find potential avenues for damage reduction. My research leverages large and heterogeneous datasets - including regulatory records, spatial information, process data - to build comprehensive models of emissions and resource use.


2. Modeling supply chains and networks

A central focus of my research is understanding sustainability challenges through the lens of networks. I model supply chains, energy systems, and material flows as interconnected networks to capture dependencies and bottlenecks. This perspective helps reveal how local decisions propagate through systems, shaping emissions, resource use, and risk.


3. Supporting energy technologies

I evaluate new energy sources and technologies, with applications in alternative fuels and critical minerals. My research uses optimization and scenario-based analysis to explore trade-offs among emissions reduction, resource availability, and resilience, with the goal of informing robust and realistic energy transition strategies.
