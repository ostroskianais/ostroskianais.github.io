Beef and environmental impacts: water (part 1)

We traced beef supply chains in the United States at the county level from ranch -> feedlot -> processing -> consumption based on an optimization framework to minimize transporation costs. We then calculated the associated "virtual water" to compare environmental impacts from the point of view of production and consumption.

Beef and environmental impacts: nitrogen (part 2)

We expanded the beef supply chain to include feed movements and calculated nitrogen budgets at every step. We obtained a network of nitrogen retained and nitrogen released. As nitrogen is one of the most important environmental impacts caused by beef consumption, people can use our network to check the counties where nitrogen was lost associated with beef consumption in their home counties.
