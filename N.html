have you ever heard of nitrogen polution?


Nitrogen is essential for life on Earth and it is vastly abundant in the form of dinitrogen (N2). Most organisms need a reactive form of nitrogen to sustain life. The Harber-Bosh process to convert dinitrogen into ammonia (NH3) changed the form we produce food for the best because we can produce large quantities and sustain a growing population. But excess nitrogen can have many effects on the environment including impacts on human health, on aquatic systems, reduction of biodiversity, and climate change.


Nitrogen is released during every stage in beef production: from fertilizer use during feed production, animal excretion, and wastewater in processing plants. In the map, you can see where that may happen relative to where the beef is consumed. The values are quantiles and refer to all nitrogen released to the environment associated with one-year worth of beef consumption in a given county. Note that this is a model that assumes companies and farmers are operating to minimize their costs, and may not reflect real links. Nonetheless, it is a great way to see how far away the pollution is.
